Nordic B2B payments infrastructure is one of the most active M&A sub-sectors in European fintech. The region's high digital adoption, mature open banking ecosystem (PSD2 penetration exceeding 70%), and fragmented mid-market vendor base have created fertile conditions for consolidation.

The sector is characterised by recurring SaaS revenue models (80–95% ARR), high switching costs driven by deep ERP integrations, and strong net revenue retention (median 118%). Strategic acquirers — primarily pan-Nordic banks and global payment processors — are competing with growth equity funds for assets.

Key verticals include account-to-account (A2A) payment rails, treasury management SaaS, cross-border reconciliation platforms, and embedded finance APIs.

Agent 04 Risk & Tailwinds
↑ PSD3 Regulatory Tailwind Low Risk
PSD3 draft implementation accelerates open banking API standardisation across the EU, expanding total addressable market for A2A infrastructure providers. Nordic markets are leading adopters.
⚠ Valuation Multiple Compression Medium
Rising ECB base rate environment (currently 3.25%) is compressing growth equity multiples. B2B SaaS at <€10m ARR increasingly scrutinised on path-to-profitability rather than topline growth.
↑ Pan-Nordic Bank Consolidation Tailwind
Nordea, SEB, and DNB have signalled intent to acquire infrastructure capabilities rather than build. This increases strategic acquirer competition for assets, supporting seller valuations.
⚠ DORA Compliance Cost Burden High Risk
EU Digital Operational Resilience Act (DORA) enforced Jan 2025. Smaller vendors (<50 FTE) face disproportionate compliance cost burden, potentially suppressing EBITDA margins in diligence periods.
